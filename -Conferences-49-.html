The events are listed in the languages that they will be held.

EDEN25 – Re-Imagining Learning Environments

Our current age has been framed by the concept of the "information age". Sometimes also known as the "computer age". In a networked society as ours, digital technology has touched and changed many aspects of day-to-day life. For example, many long-standing societal, business and institutional systems have either lost their relevance or have transformed beyond recognition, the music, banking and travel industries being excellent examples. Education does not stand untouched and we observe emerging and declining paradigms, changing expectations from society, our students now framed as consumers, with new and emerging types of informal learning experiences (take MOOCs for example) and all too frequently operating in unstable economic and policy environments.
June 14 - 15, 2016
Budapest, Hungary
Find out more

 

E-Learning@HSLU 2016 – Treffen der E-Learning Community

Nach den erfolgreichen Treffen der E-Learning Community der letzten drei Jahre möchten wir diesen Austausch über die Departementsgrenzen hinweg weiter führen. Gerne ladne wir alle an E- und Blended Learning Interessierten zum vierten Treffen ein. Es erwarten Sie Einblicke in mediengestützte Lehr/Lernprojekte. In Workshops soll der Austausch von Erfahrungen mit E-Learning gefördert werden. Zum anschliessenden Networking-Apéro sind alle herzlich eingelanden.
June 15, 2016
HSLU, Lucerne, Switzerland
Find out more

 

Appetit Webinar – Knowledge Communication in Digital Education with Videos

As a follow-up to the research video "Video Styles in MOOCs – A journey into the world of digital education" sowie die Paper Publikation "Differences & Commonalities, A comparative report of video styles and course descritptions on edX, Coursera, Futurelearn and iversity", Jeanine Reutemann will hold a webinar on knowledge transfer through educational videos. The focus will be primarily on the representation of visible speakers (educators) as well as their performance, appearance and bodily representation.
June 16, 2016
online with SWITCHinteract
Find out more

 

PBL Conference 2016

In recent years competence orientation has become a primary focus of educational debate. Problem-based learning (PBL) and related approaches such as project-based learning and enquiry-based learning are seen as promising methods of establishing competence development in educational courses, thereby providing a comprehensive basis for promoting learning processes and the skills necessary to operate effectively in everyday life. The 2016 Conference "PBL - Promoting competences, shaping the future" will yield valuable insights into current research and innovative practice projects.
June 16 - 17, 2016
Zurich University of Teacher Education, Switzerland
Find out more

 

Workshop Annotate: Wirksam lernen mit Videoanalyse: online und kollaborativ

Dieser Workshop baut auf dem Annotations- resp. Videoanalyse-Tool auf, das mit einem Opencast 2.x betrieben werden kann. Im Workshop lernen Sie bereits erprobte Einsatzszenarien der Videoanalyse kennen und erfahren, wie diese mit Hilfe des in ILIAS integrierten Annotations-Tools sinnvoll und effizient umgesetzt werden können. Der Hauptteil des Workshops ist für die konkrete Umsetzung eigener Lernarrangements reserviert: Sie arbeiten an eigenen oder an beispielhaften Szenarien und erproben dabei die Funktionen des Tools
June 21, 2016
University of Bern, Switzerland
Find out more

Webinar: Using Videos for Teaching & Learning: Scenarios based on SWITCHcast and its Moodle-Plugin

Webinar: Interactive Learning Videos with Capira

In most current online courses, quizzes are detached from the video both visually and in terms of content, and break the flow of the learner’s experience. Hence, the authors of courses may hesitate to use a large number of lightweight quizzes in their courses, even though research in didactics suggests beneficial effects in doing so. As such, we have developed a web application to turn online videos into interactive learning experiences that engage students and deepen understanding.
July 14, 2016
Online with SWITCHinteract
Find out more

 

ICALT2016 – The 16th IEEE International Conference on Advanced Learning Technologies

A new wave of technologies is disrupting innovation in the education sector now more than ever before. Phenomena and technologies such as learning analytics, cloud computing, maker spaces, 3-D printing, wearable technologies, cognitive profiling and augmented reality are just a few examples that have seen early adopters bringing new hope and optimism to the debate about the usefulness and relevance of new technologies and learning support.
July 25 - 28, 2016
The University of Texas, Austin, USA
Find out more

 

LTEC - The 5th International Workshop on Learning Technology for Education in Cloud

The rapid and constant pace of change in technology is creating both opportunities and challenges for education. The opportunities include greater access to rich, multimedia content, the use of online availability, the widespread use of mobile computing devices that can access the Internet, the expanding role of social networking tools for learning and professional development, the use of MOOC, cloud computing, big data and the growing interest in the power of digital games for more personalized learning. Besides technologies, there are also new pedagogical advances in learning and teaching. The 5th LTEC (2016) will examine these technologies and pedagogical advances that are changing the way teachers teach and students learn while giving special emphasis to the pegagogically effective ways you can harness these new technologies in education.
July 25 - 28, 2016
FernUniversität Hagen, Germany
Find out more

ECER 2016 – Leading Education: The Distinct Contributions of Educational Research and Researchers

Educational research can play a leading role in imaginatively and creatively constructing our education futures with the bricolage from past and present, and from research, policy and practice. Tensions between competing interests challenge educational researchers and practitioners alike, and give rise to the more general concern of what it means to exercise leadership, and what, as individuals and communities of practice, we stand for in such circumstances.
August 22 - 26, 2016
University College Dublin, Ireland
Find out more

 

8th Biennial Conference of EARLI SIG 1: Assessment & Evaluation – Building Bridges between Assessment and Evaluation

Our SIG was established as a platform for dialogue between scholars with a focus on classroom assessment and scholars with an orientation toward large-scale assessment and program evaluation. our theme for the 2016 conference is a reorientation to this core dialogue as it is central to issues faced by assessment in school contexts as well as by assessment in professional contexts. Doing so, we intend to build bridges between these two areas of research and practice and that through theses bridges we can explore new perspectives and approaches to assessment and evaluation.
August 24 - 26, 2016
Munich, Germany

DeLFI & HDI 2016

Die Fachgruppe E-Learning der Gesellschaft für Informatik e.V. (GI) veranstaltet seit 2003 die Tagungsreihe DeLFI: Jährlich werden dem interessierten Fachpublikum aktuelle, innovative und informatiknahe Ergebnisse zum Thema E-Learning aus Forschung und Praxis präsentiert.
